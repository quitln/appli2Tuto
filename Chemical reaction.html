A chemical reaction Ocean acidification causes the pH of seawater to drop as it absorbs CO2 from the atmosphere. Each year, humanity produces 35 billion tonnes of CO2 through its activities (such as the use of fossil fuels and deforestation). The oceans absorb up to 30% of atmospheric CO2, resulting in a chemical reaction lasting in which the concentrations of carbonate ions decrease, while the concentrations of hydrogen ions increase. This change makes the water more acidic. Since the Industrial Revolution, the pH of the oceans has dropped by 0.1 units. It may sound insignificant, but it does mean that ocean acidity has increased by about 28% since the mid-19th century. The group of intergovernmental experts on climate change (IPCC) believe that this acidification is accelerating.
